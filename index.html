About Baguio City

Baguio, officially the City of Baguio, is a 1st-class highly urbanized city in the Cordillera Administrative Region, Philippines. It is known as the "Summer Capital of the Philippines" due to its cool climate.

Established as a hill station by the Americans in the early 1900s, Baguio sits approximately 1,500 meters (4,900 feet) above sea level, nestled within the Cordillera Central mountain range in northern Luzon.

The city is rich in cultural heritage, blending indigenous Igorot traditions with Spanish and American colonial influences, making it a unique destination for both local and international tourists.
